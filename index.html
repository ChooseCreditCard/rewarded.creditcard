How Credit-Card Rewards Really Work

Reward cards convert everyday spending into points, miles or cashback. But earn rates, multipliers and redemption rules vary massively — here’s how to make sense of it.

Explore the Rewards Hub

The Basics of Credit-Card Rewards

Reward cards return value to the cardholder through points, miles or cashback on spending. Some offer flat earn rates, while others use category multipliers (travel, dining, groceries, etc.).

Higher-earning cards often charge annual fees and require strategic use to be worth it. Some rewards work best for travel, others for simple statement credits.

How You Earn Points, Miles or Cashback

1. Base Earn Rate

Every purchase earns a minimum rate — typically 1 point/mile or 1% cashback per dollar spent.

2. Category Multipliers

Many cards reward specific categories such as travel, dining, groceries or gas. These multipliers can significantly boost earning for certain lifestyles.

3. Partner Bonuses

Some cards offer extra earnings when booking through travel portals or partner merchants.

4. Intro Bonuses

Large welcome bonuses can heavily affect first-year value — but require careful attention to minimum spend requirements.

Redemption Value — Where Rewards Differ

All rewards are not equal. Cashback is simple and usually fixed value. Flexible bank points can have variable value depending on travel partners. Airline miles fluctuate depending on season, route, and seat class.

Cashback

Simple and predictable. Ideal for users who don’t optimize travel rewards.

Flexible Bank Points

Can exceed 2–3% effective value when transferred to travel partners — but require strategy.

Airline & Hotel Points

Potential for high value on premium cabin flights — but redemption rules are complex.
